# Pendidikan, Literasi dan Pelatihan

Pendidikan merupakan salah satu pilar atau fokus dalam kebijakan kami terhadap pengembangan masyarakat. Pendidikan merupakan modal penting bagi setiap individu untuk mencapai cita-citanya. Pendidikan merupakan hak mendasar yang harus dipenuhi. Pendidikan juga merupakan faktor penting dalam pembentukan manusia tangguh guna mencapai kemajuan bangsa.

- MPMulia as well as Honda communities in East Java distributed donation under Honda Community Social Responsibility (HCSR) of SATU HATI Bersama Indonesia Sehat campaign, which targeted at medical staffs in 12 cities in East Java. The donation was collected by selling #MboisituDiRumahAja and Fight Back Covid-19 T-shirts. Donation was made in the forms of 2,000 disposable masks, 2,000 gloves, 1,000 face shields, 8,000 tablets of multivitamin and 1,500 packs of full cream UHT milk.
- Service and engine oil replacement were free during April to June 2020, applied to all medical staffs from 15 Reference Hospitals in East Java. A total of 900 Honda motorcycles belonging to medical staffs signed up for free service and engine oil replacement. To support this activity, the Company spent Rp137,915,579.

Total expenses for those health facilities aimed at helping to tackle Covid-19 amounted to Rp10,788,174,694.

Support for education is not only realized for formal institutions but also through literacy and skills training in order to get certain insights and skills that useful for their lives. Here are the steps that the Company has taken to advance the quality of education:

- Digital Asset Donation and Digital Class
During the Covid-19 pandemic, all activities have been adjusted to minimize the impact of Covid-19. Without exception, teaching and learning process also should be done via online or Distant Learning. Seeing that condition, the Company realized that not all children can follow online learning adequately due to the limited access to supporting infrastructure. Therefore, the Company provided assistance for 624 fostered children of SOS Children's Village in Jakarta, Bogor, and Maumere (Flores), in the forms of laptops, smart TVs, printers, projectors and screen projectors, signal amplifiers, mouse, USB, and folding tables. The Company also facilitated digital literacy classes for these children to learn more about basic computer science, coding, and software with fun learning methods. The implementation of this program aimed at improving children's digital literacy skills, especially for Z and Alpha generations who are required to be more tech savvy. Total fund spent for this activity was amounted to Rp113,000,000 (one hundred thirteen million Rupiah).
