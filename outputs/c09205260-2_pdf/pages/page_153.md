** Lost workday case rate is the number of work-related injuries that result in time away from work per 100 employees and contractors that HP manages working a full year. Rates are calculated globally using the US Occupational Safety and Health Administration (OSHA) definitions for recordability and OSHA calculation methodologies. The figures are based on employees working an average of 2,000 hours during a full year. The US average in 2021 (the most recent data available) for the Computer and Peripheral Equipment Manufacturing Industry (NAICS Code 33411) was 0.1. Data are for the calendar year.
*** Recordable incidence rate is the number of work-related injury cases requiring more than first aid per 100 employees and contractors that HP manages. Rates are calculated globally using OSHA definitions for recordability and OSHA calculation methodologies. The figures are based on employees working an average of 2,000 hours during a full year. The US average in 2021 (the most recent data available) for the Computer and Peripheral Equipment Manufacturing Industry (NAICS Code 33411) was 0.3. Data are for the calendar year.
*** Lost time injury severity rate is the number of days lost due to injury per 100 employees and contractors that HP manages. Rates are calculated globally using OSHA definitions for recordability and OSHA calculation methodologies. The figures are based on employees working an average of 2,000 hours during a full year. Data are for the calendar year.
